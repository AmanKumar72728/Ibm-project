Save the Planet : Environment and Ecological Issues

Here are some ways to help save the planet from environmental issues:

  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle: Reusing items gives them a second life and reduces the need for new resources.
    Recycling waste into resources reduces greenhouse gas emissions and protects wildlife.
  • Conserve water and electricity: You can also try composting.
  • Be car-conscious: Walk, bike, or take public transit instead of driving.
  • Eat sustainable foods





Problem Outcome :-


  1. Pollution :
    2. Problem: Air, water, and soil contamination from industrial agricultural, and urban activities.
    Outcome: Health issues such as respiratory problems, waterborne diseases, and loss of biodiversity.

  2. Deforestation:
    3. Problem: Large-scale clearing of forests for agriculture, logging, and urban development.
    Outcome: Loss of biodiversity, disruption of water cycles, increased CO2 levels, and soil erosion.

  3. Biodiversity Loss:
    4. Problem: Extinction of species due to habitat destruction, climate change, pollution, and overexploitation.
    Outcome: Ecosystem imbalance, loss of ecosystem services, and reduced resilience to environmental changes.

  4. Resource Depletion:
    5. Problem: Overconsumption and unsustainable use of natural resources like water, minerals, and fossil fuels.
    Outcome: Resource scarcity, increased conflict over resources, and economic instability.

  5. Ocean Acidification.
    6. : Problem: Increased CO2 absorption by oceans, leading to lower pH levels.
    Outcome: Harm to marine life, particularly shellfish and coral reefs, affecting the entire marine food web

  6. Soil Degradation: .
    7. Problem: Loss of soil fertility due to erosion, overgrazing, deforestation, and chemical use.
    Outcome: Decreased agricultural productivity, food insecurity, and desertification.

Climate Change Short Video

  • Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns.
  • Such shifts can be natural, due to changes in the sun’s activity or large volcanic eruptions.
    • But since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil and gas.

    Burning fossil fuels generates greenhouse gas emissions that act like a blanket wrapped around the Earth, trapping the sun’s heat and raising temperatures.
    The main greenhouse gases that are causing climate change include carbon dioxide and methane. These come from using gasoline for driving a car or coal for heating a building, for example. Clearing land and cutting down forests can also release carbon dioxide. Agriculture, oil and gas operations are major sources of methane emissions. Energy, industry, transport, buildings, agriculture and land use are among the main sectors causing greenhouse gases.
